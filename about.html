Our Story

At InVeXodus® we are passionately committed to providing accessible training and job placement for those confronting economic hardships. Our offerings include virtual reality training and a structured UI/UX design program tailored for underserved populations, including individuals who are incarcerated or in the process of reentry. Our team brings a wealth of knowledge and experience from esteemed universities such as Stanford University, Cornell University, Columbia University, and MIT.

Central to our journey is Ryan Hopkins, a resilient individual currently incarcerated, who not only earned a BA in Liberal Studies but also pursued an MS in Industrial-Organizational Psychology. During his work on an evaluation project with RTI International, he recognized the urgent need to advocate for systems change, enabling individuals with a record to enter STEM careers. His journey took a pivotal turn when he was invited to speak at a STEM job fair organized by Prison Cells to PhD, where he met Jodi Anderson Jr., a fellow speaker and passionate tech enthusiast.

Their serendipitous meeting sparked a dynamic collaboration that led to the launch of an innovative pilot program at the Maine State Prison, in partnership with Stanford University. This initiative focused on design thinking, product development, and product marketing. One standout graduate, Christopher Laliberte, showed exceptional promise and a passion for technology and social change. Chris and Ryan engaged in countless discussions, exploring how their academic knowledge and experiences could address pressing societal issues.

Recognizing the need to bring additional depth to their mission, Chris, Jodi, and Ryan sought out Victoria Scott, who was also currently incarcerated, and whose stellar reputation preceded her. As a Justice Studies student at the University of Maine Augusta, she was known for her interdisciplinary approach and commitment to transformative systems planning. After a single conversation, it was clear that her vision and expertise were a perfect fit for InVeXodus®. Victoria’s extensive background, including her role as a teaching assistant at MIT and her successful grant writing for UMA’s Prison Education Partnership, brought invaluable insights and further strengthened our mission.

The collective experiences of Ryan, Jodi, Chris, and Victoria–both before and during incarceration–highlighted the significant employment challenges faced by justice-impacted individuals. Their deep empathy for others in similar situations fueled their determination to make a difference, as they recognized the profound impact unemployment can have on lives. Through collaborative efforts with numerous virtual reality and tech companies, the vision for InVeXodus® began to crytalize.

Our mission is clear: to empower individuals with the skills necessary to thrive in the modern workforce by dismantling barriers to employment and nurturing a supportive community. At InVeXodus®, we understand the struggles of securing meaningful careers, and we are unwavering in our commitment to equip individuals with the tools to overcome these challenges. Join us in our mission to transform lives and create lasting change.

Our Values

Integrity - Transparency - Equanimity - Innovation -Stewardship

At InVeXodus®, our core values guide our actions and decisions, ensuring we remain true to our mission and vision. These values are the foundation of our commitment to our community and stakeholders.

  • Integrity - Acting with honesty and upholding ethical standards
  • Transparency - Openly sharing processes, decisions, and financials
  • Equanimity - Fostering a balanced and supportive environment
  • Innovation - Seeking creative solutions to social challenges
  • Stewardship - Managing resources responsibly and sustainably
