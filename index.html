Operation Access Opportunity (OAO)

Designed to serve populations facing systemic employment and career advancement barriers. OAO addresses this issue by leveraging VR and other technologies to provide skill training that aligns with workforce needs. By targeting labor market demands, OAO aims to fill these job openings, thereby stabilizing and strengthening Maine's economy while providing economic mobility to populations who face systemic barriers.

CatalysTech Designs

Designed as a workforce development and economic mobility program, this initiative serves justice-impacted individuals, including those currently incarcerated and those in reentry. The program focuses on essential skill development, mentoring, career pathways, entrepreneurship, and hands-on work experiences in software engineering and UX/UI design, thereby enhancing employability outcomes.

Different ways we partner

We offer a variety of partnership opportunities to support our mission of workforce development and economic mobility.

1) Our Contract Partnership allows us to provide high-quality software development services through our CatalysTech Designs program.

2) The Education & Training Partnership focuses on leveraging learning materials and resources to deliver hands-on training in fields like software engineering and UX/UI design.

3) Through our Employment Partnership, we connect qualified individuals trained by InVeXodus® with employers seeking skilled professionals.

4) Lastly, our Mutual Interest Partnership enables us to collaborate with organizations that share our commitment to education and career development.

Have another idea for partnering with us? We’re always open to exploring new opportunities to further our mission.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is InVeXodus?

InVeXodus is an innovative workforce development and economic mobility nonprofit that leverages technology to create employment opportunities for individuals facing employment barriers.

What are the main programs offered by InVeXodus?

InVeXodus offers two main programs: Operation Access Opportunity (OAO) and CatalysTech Designs. Both programs focus on equipping participants with essential skills for modern careers through advanced technologies and hands-on training.

What is Operation Access Opportunity (OAO)?

OAO is a program designed to help individuals facing employment barriers, such as those with criminal records, low-income youth, veterans, and persons with substance use disorders. It leverages virtual reality (VR) and other innovative technologies to provide skill development for in-demand careers.

How does OAO address Maine's workforce gap?

OAO addresses Maine's workforce gap by providing skill development for in-demand careers, particularly in response to the state's aging population. The program aims to fill job openings and stabilize the economy by offering opportunities to underserved communities.

What is CatalysTech Designs?

CatalysTech Designs is a workforce development program focused on justice-impacted individuals, including those currently incarcerated and those reentering society. It offers essential skill development in software engineering and UI/UX design.

How does CatalysTech Designs help justice-impacted individuals?

CatalysTech Designs helps justice-impacted individuals by providing them with the tools needed for economic stability, including mentoring, career pathways, and hands-on work experiences. The program addresses high unemployment rates and economic challenges faced by individuals who are formerly incarcerated.

What types of partnerships does InVeXodus offer?

InVeXodus offers four types of partnerships:

  • Mutual Interest Partnerships - Organizations that share similar initiatives.
  • Employment Partnerships - Companies that recruit individuals trained from our programs for employment.
  • Contract Partnerships - Organizations that contract our services to develop products.
  • Education Partnerships - Institutions offering credentialed curriculum for our programs.
