Our Two Programs

Operation Access Opportunity (OAO) and CatalysTech Designs are transformative workforce development programs aimed at equipping underserved populations, including justice-impacted individuals and those facing systemic employment barriers, with essential skills for modern careers. By leveraging advanced technologies like virtual reality and providing hands-on training in fields such as software engineering and UX/UI design, these programs address critical labor shortages and prepare participants for in-demand jobs. Through strategic partnerships with employers and educational institutions, both initiatives offer mentoring, career pathways, and real-world work experiences, ensuring participants gain the credentials and confidence needed for economic stability. By filling workforce gaps and fostering economic mobility, these programs not only enhance individual livelihoods but also contribute to the overall economic growth and resilience of communities.

Our Programs

Operation Access Opportunity

Operation Access Opportunity (OAO) is a program designed to help individuals facing employment barriers, such as those with criminal records, low-income youth, veterans, and persons with substance use disorders. By leveraging virtual reality (VR) and other technologies, OAO provides skill development for in-demand careers, addressing Maine’s workforce gap caused by an aging population. The program aims to fill job openings, stabilize the economy, and offer opportunities for underserved populations, including remote education and training in prisons. The VR software developed will be owned by InVeXodus, ensuring it can be provided without profit margins.

CatalysTech Designs

CatalysTech Designs is a transformative workforce development program focused on justice-impacted individuals, including those currently incarcerated and those reentering society. By offering essential skill development in software engineering and UX/UI design, along with mentoring, career pathways, and hands-on work experiences, the program prepares participants for successful careers in the tech industry. CatalysTech Designs addresses the high unemployment rates and economic challenges faced by formerly incarcerated individuals by providing them with the tools needed for economic stability. Through partnerships with the Maine Department of Corrections, the program ensures that training aligns with prison system standards while fostering hope and a more normalized lifestyle for participants. InVeXodus is actively seeking companies to partner with us to employ our trained UI/UX designers and engineers, and to contract work to InVeXodus, further supporting our mission to create economic opportunities for justice-impacted individuals.
